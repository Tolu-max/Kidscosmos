Adjectives

Adjectives

  • written by KidsCosmos Team

Adjectives are describing words.

Study the words printed in bold type below:


Blind men, swinging tail, tame elephant, broad, sturdy side, squirming trunk, round, smooth, sharp tusk.

These words describe the nouns after them. Such words are called adjectives: Because they add something to the meaning of nouns or pronouns.

  1. Adjectives of quality: a kind boy, a fat cow, a black dog, a good pen.
  2. Adjectives of quantity: e.g ten girls =, both men, many cars, much water.
  3. Adjectives of distinction: e.g my book, that house, which boy, each child, either man.

Comparison Of Adjectives

Their are three degrees of comparison:

  1. Positive- the simple adjective: e.g a long river, a fat goat, a tall tree.
  2. Comparative- compares only two things. This often formed by adding er to the positive, sometimes with minor changes in spellings: e.g a longer river, a fatter goat, a taller tree.
  3. Superlative- compares more than two things. This is often formed by adding est to the positive, sometimes with minor changes in spellings, e.g the longest river, the fattest goat, the tallest tree.

Study the examples below

# Positive Comparative Superlative
1 easy easier easiest
2 quick quicker quickest
3 Happy Happier Happiest
4 Hot Hotter Hottest
5 Long Longer longest
6 Small Smaller Smallest
7 Thin Thinner Thinnest
8 Fast Faster Fastest
9 Great Greater Greatest
10 Slow Slower Slowest
11 Big Bigger Biggest
12 Loud Louder Loudest
13 Busy Busier Busiest
14 Wise Wiser Wisest
15 Large Larger largest
Note the changes in the spelling of the words printed boldly in the above examples

More and Most are added before the positive of words of two or more than two syllables to form the comparative and the superlative:

# Positive Comparative Superlative
1 Beautiful more beautiful most beautiful
2 intelligent more intelligent most intelligent
3 careful more careful most careful
4 comfortable more comfortable most comfortable
5 difficult more difficult most difficult
6 Wonderful more Wonderful most Wonderful
7 careless more careless most careless

Certain adjectives require different words to form the comparative and the superlative: e.g

# Positive Comparative Superlative
1 good better best
2 old older, elder oldest, eldest
3 bad worse worst
4 up upper uppermost
5 difficult more difficult most difficult
6 Wonderful more Wonderful most Wonderful
7 careless more careless most careless

Some adjectives cannot be compared because of their fixed meaning. For example it will be absurd to say: square, squarer,squarest. Once a figure is a square, it is a square.
