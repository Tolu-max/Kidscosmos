Nouns

All About Nouns

  • Written by KidsCosmos Team

Here, we are going to talk about basics of Nouns

Study the twelve words printed in bold type below:
Alexandra, hospital, bread, doctor, money, home, street, purse, car, driver, life, naira.

These words are all names.
Alexandra, doctor, driver, refer to persons.
Hospital, home, are the names of places.
Bread, money, street, naira, etc. are the names of things. Such words which are names of persons, places or things are called Nouns.

"You should try to learn as much as you can and also understand it."

SINGULAR AND PLURAL NOUNS

A noun which refers to one person, animal, place or thing is singular; e.g. man, goat, church, basket. A noun which refers to more than one person, animal, place or thing is plural; e.g. men, goats, churches, baskets. We can form plurals in various ways:

  1. By adding 's'

    # Singular Plural
    1 cat cats
    2 roof roofs
    3 day days
    4 key keys
    5 week weeks
    6 plate plates
    7 chief chiefs
    8 boy boys
    9 book books
    10 cloth cloths

    Note: The words cloth and cloths refer to pieces of material. Clothes are what we wear and the word is never written in the singular.

  2. By adding 'es'

    # Singular Plural
    1 church churches
    2 potato potatoes
    3 gas gases
    4 hero heroes
    5 brush brushes
    6 horse horses
    7 box boxes
    8 watch watches
    9 tomato tomatoes
    10 echo echoes

  3. By changing 'y' into 'i' before adding 'es'

    # Singular Plural
    1 lady ladies
    2 baby babies
    3 army armies
    4 fly flies
    5 city cities
    6 body bodies

  4. By changing 'f' into 'v' before adding 'es'

    # Singular Plural
    1 leaf leaves
    2 shelf shelves
    3 life lives
    4 calf calves
    5 half halves
    6 loaf loaves
    7 self selves
    8 thief thieves
    9 wolf wolves

  5. By changing one or more vowels

    # Singular Plural
    1 man men
    2 mouse mice
    3 oasis oases
    4 goose geese
    5 tooth teeth
    6 woman women
    7 foot feet

  6. By adding 'en'

    # Singular Plural
    1 ox oxen
    2 child children

  7. Some words do not change

    # Singular Plural
    1 sheep sheep
    2 swine swine
    3 dear dear
    4 trout trout
    5 salmon salmon
    6 cod cod
    7 cannon cannon
    8 dozen dozen
    9 gross gross

  8. Compound Words

    # Singular Plural
    1 passer-by passers-by
    2 step-child step-children
    3 washer-man washer-men
    4 son-in-law sons-in-law
    5 step-daughter step-daughters
    6 man-servant man-servants
    7 commander-in-chief commanders-in-chief
    8 police-constable police-constables
    9 man-of-war men-of-war
    10 looker-on lookers-on

  9. A few words have no singulars:
    Scissors, pliers, trousers, spectacles, riches, alms, measles, thanks, pincers, clothes.

  10. Plurals of Proper Nouns

    # Singular Plural
    1 Andrew Andrews
    2 Mark Marks
    3 Mr. White Mr. Whites
    4 Master John Masters Johns
    5 Mrs. Mary Mrs. Marys
    6 Miss Dora Misses Doras

  11. Some nouns have two forms of plural

    # Singular Plural
    1 penny pennies, pence
    2 pea peas, pease
    3 genius geniuses, genii
    4 brother brothers, brethren
    5 fish fishes, fish
    6 shot shots, shot
    7 person persons, people
